Managing bloating & comfort
🍽️
Eat slowly, chew well
Digestion starts in the mouth. Rushed eating is one of the biggest causes of bloating. Aim for 15–20 chews per mouthful.
🚶
Short walk after meals
Even 8–10 minutes of walking after eating improves digestion and reduces bloating for many people.
🌿
Peppermint or ginger tea
Both have good evidence for easing bloating. A cup after dinner is a simple, effective habit.

Your core principles for steady energy
🐟
Oily fish 3× per week
Salmon, mackerel, sardines — natural omega-3 that works alongside your supplement and supports recovery.
🫐
Colour on every plate
Berries, leafy greens, colourful veg — polyphenols support brain function and reduce oxidative stress that contributes to fatigue.
🥚
Protein at every meal
Eggs, fish, meat, legumes — keeps blood sugar stable so energy doesn't crash mid-morning or afternoon.
🌿
Liver-friendly choices
Cruciferous veg (broccoli, kale) and garlic support your liver's natural detox pathways — pairs well with milk thistle.
🍞
Slow carbs, not no carbs
Oats, sweet potato, brown rice — sustained energy without the spikes and crashes that feel like fatigue.
🧈
Take supplements with fat
Milk thistle and omega-3 absorb much better with a meal containing fat (avocado, olive oil, nuts, eggs, or oily fish).
🍷
Alcohol is the biggest lever
Even moderate amounts affect deep sleep, recovery, and next-day energy. Alcohol-free evenings are one of the highest-impact habits you can build.
🦠
Feed your gut daily
Live yoghurt or kefir + prebiotic fibres (garlic, oats, onions) = better microbiome diversity and more consistent energy for many people.

Why consistent sleep matters for energy
Deep sleep is when your body does most of its repair and recovery work. Consistent bedtimes, magnesium, no alcohol, and a cool dark room are the highest-leverage things most people can control. Small improvements here often show up as noticeably better energy within a week or two.
