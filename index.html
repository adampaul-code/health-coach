Why this matters for you
Your gut microbiome directly influences your brain fog and neurological fatigue via the gut-brain axis. With spina bifida, vagus nerve function can affect gut motility — explaining your bloating and irregular digestion. A healthier microbiome means better neurotransmitter production, reduced systemic inflammation, and more consistent energy.
Probiotic foods you'll enjoy
🥛
Kefir (start here)
Tastes like thin, slightly tangy yoghurt. 150ml per day. Contains 30+ bacterial strains. Start with 50ml and build up.
🫙
Live Greek yoghurt daily
Must say "live cultures" on the label. Activia, Fage, or Yeo Valley organic are good UK options.
🧀
Aged cheese
Mature cheddar, parmesan, and gouda all contain live cultures. Already in your diet probably — now you know it counts.
🍵
Kombucha
Fizzy, slightly sweet fermented tea. GT's Synergy or Equinox widely available in UK supermarkets. Check sugar — under 6g per 100ml.
🍜
Miso
Stir a teaspoon into hot (not boiling) water. Mild savoury flavour — nothing like fermented vegetables.
Prebiotic foods — feed your bacteria
🧅

🌾
Oats every morning possible

🍌
Slightly underripe bananas

🥦
Broccoli and asparagus
Both prebiotic and liver-supportive simultaneously. Already in your meal plan — now doing gut work too.
Managing bloating
🍽️
Eat slowly, chew thoroughly
Digestion begins in the mouth. Rushed eating is a primary cause of bloating — aim for 20 chews per mouthful.
🚶
Short walk after meals
Even 10 minutes walking after eating significantly improves gut motility — particularly relevant given vagus nerve involvement.
🌿
Peppermint or ginger tea
Both have clinical evidence for reducing bloating and improving gut motility. After dinner — replaces the desire for something sweet.

Your eating principles
🐟
Oily fish 3× per week
Salmon, mackerel, sardines — natural EPA and DHA that amplify your omega-3 supplement and reduce neurological inflammation.
🫐
Colour on every plate
Berries, leafy greens, and brightly coloured vegetables provide polyphenols that support brain function and reduce fatigue-driving oxidative stress.
🥚
Protein at every meal
Eggs, fish, meat, legumes — protein stabilises blood sugar, preventing the energy crashes that compound neurological fatigue.
🌿
Liver-friendly choices
Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, kale, Brussels sprouts) and garlic support your liver's detox pathways — complementing your milk thistle directly.
🍞
Slow carbs, not no carbs
Oats, sweet potato, brown rice — sustained energy without spikes. Avoid refined carbs mid-morning which cause crashes that look like fatigue.
🧈
Fat with your supplements
Your milk thistle phytosome and omega-3 both absorb significantly better with a meal containing fat.
🍷
Alcohol is the biggest lever
Even one drink suppresses deep sleep by 20–30%, elevates neurological inflammation, and works directly against your milk thistle. Every AF day is a real gain.
🦠
Feed your gut daily
Live yoghurt, kefir, or kombucha for probiotics. Garlic, oats, and onions for prebiotic fibre. Both needed — probiotics without prebiotics don't survive.

Why sleep matters most for you
With spina bifida-related fatigue, deep sleep is where neurological repair happens, growth hormone is released, and the glymphatic system clears inflammatory waste from your brain. Poor sleep directly worsens brain fog, compensation fatigue, and your body's ability to benefit from your supplement stack. Magnesium, no alcohol, cool room, dark environment, and consistent sleep and wake times are your four biggest levers.
